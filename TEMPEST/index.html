Ornithopters
The Power of Flight

An ornithopter is a mechanical device that has wings powered by rubberbands, helping it fly. An ornithopter is typically made of wood, but can also be made of plastic. It is usually made of a light material, which helps it fly better. I had to look at pictures of other ornithopters and also had to draw my ideas in my notebook to form a solid plan for this project. I used light pieces of wood to make the frame of the ornithopter, while using straws for the frame of the wings and tail wing. I used wire to make the crank shaft, which was very hard to create. After I finished building it, I had to also test if the ornithopter could actually fly.

Making the Crankshaft

The crankshaft was the hardest part to make out of the whole ornithopter. The crank shaft was hard to design because I had to design it so the wings would flap symmetrically and not at the wrong times. I made the crank shaft out of popsicle sticks and drew a wire through the popsicle sticks to form the actual crank that you spin. It took a few tries and a LOT of popsicle sticks, but I finally made it work by bending the wire in a way so when you crank it, the wings move symmetrically.
